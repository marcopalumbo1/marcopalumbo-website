Biografia

Marco Palumbo, Classe 2002. Affascinato dalla musica fin da piccolo, si innamora del basso durante la scuola media e decide di approfondire lo studio prendendo lezioni di strumento sotto la guida del Maestro Valerio Bruno, in arte Combass, bassista/produttore degli Après La Classe e turnista di fama nazionale e internazionale. Con lui continua gli studi per 4 anni, affrontando tutte le sfaccettature dello strumento e trattando tutti i generi musicali: Pop, Rock, Reggae, Jazz, Blues, Motown ecc. Negli anni amplia le sue conoscenze musicali collaborando con numerosi colleghi e formazioni artistiche. Nel 2020 entra nel Locomotive Jazz Festival, con il quale si esibisce in più occasioni aprendo concerti di artisti come Simona Molinari. Ha la possibilità di esibirsi anche in Francia, conoscere nuovi artisti e arricchire il proprio bagaglio musicale. Nel 2021 partecipa a Battiti Live (Italia1) come bassista di Tancredi. Nello stesso anno entra nei Kamafei, gruppo storico salentino che fonde musica popolare e strumenti moderni. Con loro gira l’Italia e prende parte alle tappe de La Notte della Taranta. Entra nei MamaSka (ska/reggae/rocksteady) e partecipa a Sanremo Rock 2021, esibendosi all’Ariston e classificandosi 2° su 12.000 band, vincendo il premio come miglior brano radiofonico. Registra diverse linee di basso in studio per la band. Nel 2022 partecipa alla presentazione del disco “Sarai Bellissima” di Imperfetto Prossimo sul palco dei Teatini (Lecce), in onore di Paolo Borsellino. Registra per loro “Turbolenta Marea”, premiata dalla FIMMG con il premio “Mario Boni”. Con la band apre il concerto a Franco Simone al Teatro Apollo. Nel 2023 fonda i Joaniti (funk/R&B), suonando in numerosi locali e collaborando con Mylious Johnson (P!nk, Destiny’s Child, Mariah Carey, Giorgia, Ultimo...). Diventa bassista di Leo Gaballo, giovane promessa neretina, registrando e suonando in vari eventi. Nel 2024 diventa bassista ufficiale dell’orchestra del Li Ucci Festival. Studia al Conservatorio “Nino Rota” con il Maestro Mario Guarini, attuale bassista di Claudio Baglioni. Registra il musical “Polo Nord” (20+ brani) per Mind Production, in tour dal 2024. Lavora al disco 2025/2026 dei Kamafei. Collabora a progetti originali come quello di Frank Bramato e registra "Paradiso Di Notte" della Sottoscritta, con Mylious Johnson. Coltiva la propria presenza social, collaborando con aziende come Moises, nota app musicale mondiale.

Biography

Marco Palumbo, born in 2002, has been fascinated by music since childhood. He fell in love with the bass during middle school and decided to deepen his studies by taking lessons with Maestro Valerio Bruno, also known as Combass — bassist and producer of Après La Classe and renowned session musician. Marco studied under his guidance for four years, exploring all the nuances of the instrument and covering a wide range of musical genres including Pop, Rock, Reggae, Jazz, Blues, and Motown. Over the years, Marco expanded his musical knowledge by collaborating with numerous colleagues and artistic ensembles. In 2020, he joined the Locomotive Jazz Festival, performing on several occasions and opening concerts for major artists such as Simona Molinari. This opportunity allowed him to travel abroad, perform in France, meet artists, and broaden his cultural horizons. In 2021, he performed at Battiti Live (broadcast on Italia1) as the bassist for Tancredi. That same year, he joined the historic Salento group Kamafei, known for blending traditional folk music with modern instrumentation. With Kamafei, he toured Italy performing in public squares and took part in the annual Festival La Notte della Taranta. Marco also became a member of MamaSka, a ska/reggae/rocksteady band. He participated in Sanremo Rock 2021, performing at the Ariston Theatre and placing second out of 12,000 bands, earning the award for Best Radio Track. He has worked in studio sessions on multiple occasions, recording several basslines for MamaSka. In 2022, he performed at the presentation of the album "Sarai Bellissima" by Imperfetto Prossimo at the Teatini stage (Lecce) in honor of Paolo Borsellino. For this group, he recorded bass on tracks including "Turbolenta Marea," which won the “Mario Boni” award from FIMMG, among others. With Imperfetto Prossimo, he also opened a concert at the Apollo Theatre in Lecce for Franco Simone. In 2023, Marco founded Joaniti, a group of young musicians with a funk/R&B soul. Together, they performed in various venues and had the chance to collaborate with internationally acclaimed drummer Mylious Johnson — known for working with P!nk, Destiny’s Child, Mariah Carey, Quincy Jones, as well as Italian artists like Giorgia, Jovanotti, and Ultimo. He became the bassist for Leo Gaballo, a promising young artist from Nardò, recording several tracks and performing in theaters and public events. In 2024, he was named the official bassist for the Li Ucci Festival orchestra, performing in numerous events and in the final concert. Marco is currently attending the “Nino Rota” Conservatory in Monopoli, studying under Maestro Mario Guarini — a highly respected bassist currently playing for Claudio Baglioni. His dedication to both original and cover projects continues to grow steadily. In late 2024, he recorded the musical “Polo Nord” (over twenty songs) for Mind Production, which is scheduled to tour various Italian theaters and cinemas. In 2025, Marco is actively working in the studio on the 2025/2026 Kamafei album. He is gaining increasing recognition among local musicians and is being involved in several projects, including an original production by Frank Bramato. He also contributed to the recording of "Paradiso Di Notte" by Sottoscritta, which features Mylious Johnson. Beyond his live performances and studio work, Marco is invested in his digital presence, leveraging social media platforms to grow his visibility. Thanks to this, he has caught the attention of several brands and has started collaborating with them — including the globally renowned music app Moises.
